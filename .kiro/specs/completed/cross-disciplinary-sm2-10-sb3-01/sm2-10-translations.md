## Question 1: Rhine River Temperature and Fish Diversity

### English
**Prompt:** Basel's Rhine River monitoring shows summer temperature: mean μ = 21.5°C, SD σ = 2.3°C (normal distribution). Fish diversity (Shannon index) correlates with temperature at r = -0.72. Calculate P(T > 24°C).

**Hint 1:** Calculate z-score: z = (x - μ) / σ

**Hint 2:** Use z-table or normal distribution: P(z > 1.087) ≈ 0.138

**Hint 3:** Strong negative correlation means higher temperature reduces diversity significantly

**Explanation:** z = (24 - 21.5) / 2.3 = 1.087. From normal distribution, P(z > 1.087) = 0.138 or 13.8%. The strong negative correlation (r = -0.72) indicates that when temperature exceeds 24°C (13.8% probability), fish diversity decreases substantially, threatening Rhine ecosystem health.

## Question 2: Urban Park Biodiversity Comparison

### English
**Prompt:** Kannenfeldpark bird surveys (n=20): mean = 32 species, SD = 4.2. Novartis Campus (n=20): mean = 28 species, SD = 3.8. Calculate the lower bound of the 95% confidence interval for the difference in means.

**Hint 1:** Calculate difference in means: 32 - 28 = 4 species

**Hint 2:** SE of difference = √[(4.2²/20) + (3.8²/20)] = 1.267

**Hint 3:** 95% CI: difference ± 1.96(SE), find lower bound

**Explanation:** Difference = 4 species. SE = √[(4.2²/20) + (3.8²/20)] = 1.267. 95% CI: 4 ± 1.96(1.267) = 4 ± 2.48, giving (1.52, 6.48). Lower bound = 1.52 species. This indicates Kannenfeldpark has significantly higher bird diversity than Novartis Campus, likely due to larger habitat area and vegetation diversity.

## Question 3: Climate Trend Analysis

### English
**Prompt:** Basel temperature: 9.2°C (1990) to 11.0°C (2024), 34 years. Growing season was 165 days (1990), increases 8 days per °C. Calculate 2024 growing season length.

**Hint 1:** Calculate total temperature increase: 11.0 - 9.2 = 1.8°C

**Hint 2:** Calculate growing season increase: 1.8°C × 8 days/°C

**Hint 3:** Add to 1990 baseline: 165 + increase

**Explanation:** Temperature increase = 1.8°C over 34 years. Growing season increase = 1.8 × 8 = 14.4 days. 2024 growing season = 165 + 14.4 = 179.4 days. This 14-day extension allows earlier spring plant growth and later autumn activity, significantly impacting Basel's urban ecosystems and agricultural practices.

## Question 4: Population Dynamics Probability

### English
**Prompt:** Basel urban fox population: mean K = 150, SD = 18 (normal distribution). Safe range: 120-180 individuals. Calculate probability population falls outside safe range.

**Hint 1:** Calculate z-scores for both boundaries: z = (x - μ) / σ

**Hint 2:** z₁ = (120 - 150) / 18 = -1.667, z₂ = (180 - 150) / 18 = 1.667

**Hint 3:** P(outside) = P(X < 120) + P(X > 180)

**Explanation:** z₁ = -1.667, P(X < 120) = 0.048. z₂ = 1.667, P(X > 180) = 0.048. Total probability = 0.048 + 0.048 = 0.096 or 9.6%. This means Basel's fox population has ~10% chance of requiring wildlife management intervention due to resource stress or genetic concerns.

## Question 5: Ecosystem Confidence Interval

### English
**Prompt:** Rhine River dissolved oxygen (n=25): mean = 9.8 mg/L, SD = 1.4 mg/L. Fish require ≥ 8.0 mg/L. Calculate lower bound of 90% confidence interval.

**Hint 1:** Calculate standard error: SE = SD / √n = 1.4 / √25

**Hint 2:** 90% CI uses z = 1.645: mean ± 1.645(SE)

**Hint 3:** Find lower bound and compare to 8.0 mg/L threshold

**Explanation:** SE = 1.4 / 5 = 0.28. 90% CI: 9.8 ± 1.645(0.28) = 9.8 ± 0.461, giving (9.34, 10.26) mg/L. Lower bound = 9.34 mg/L, which exceeds the 8.0 mg/L threshold. The Rhine is safe for fish populations with 90% confidence, indicating good water quality management.
